# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

The primary users are trained Royal Glass staff performing preliminary residential pool screening for Auckland properties. They need to move quickly from an entered address to an understandable assessment while retaining access to the evidence, limitations, and follow-up work behind that assessment.

## Product Purpose

The product gives staff a fast, evidence-led first assessment of whether an Auckland property has credible potential for a residential swimming pool. It resolves the property, presents official mapped evidence and deterministic screening results, keeps unknowns explicit, and identifies what still requires onsite investigation or specialist review.

Success means a staff member can enter an address, understand the preliminary assessment quickly, expand supporting sections when more detail is needed, and download a client-ready PDF report without the product overstating certainty. Provider-assisted address entry should reduce input friction while preserving authoritative property identity; LINZ is the current official source, while any Google integration requires separate accuracy, licensing, privacy, and reconciliation decisions.

The current implementation remains an internal-only, Auckland-only, no-database, session-scoped proof of concept. PDF reporting, expandable result sections, and enhanced address search are intended product direction rather than claims about current capability.

## Positioning

A fast, evidence-led first look at residential pool potential, grounded in official Auckland property data and honest about what still requires onsite investigation.

## Brand Personality

Trustworthy, clear, and pragmatic. The product should feel calm and professionally credible, helping staff make sense of complex property evidence without making the screening result appear more certain or authoritative than it is.

## Anti-references

The interface must not resemble a flashy consumer sales funnel, a generic SaaS dashboard, or an official Council approval system. It should avoid decorative complexity, sales-led urgency, and visual treatments that could make preliminary screening appear to be consent, engineering, survey, title, utility-location, or construction-safety advice.

## Design Principles

1. **Lead with the decision, preserve the evidence.** Make the preliminary outcome easy to understand, with expandable detail available for verification.
2. **Make uncertainty visible.** Clearly distinguish verified evidence, unavailable information, assumptions, and required follow-up.
3. **Optimize for a staff workflow.** Reduce effort from address entry through assessment review and report download without hiding consequential choices.
4. **Earn trust through provenance.** Keep official sources, attribution, property identity, and calculation boundaries visible wherever they affect a finding.
5. **Produce a responsible handoff.** On-screen results and downloadable reports must remain useful to staff and clients without presenting preliminary screening as approval or professional certification.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Support complete keyboard operation, visible focus, labelled controls, polite progress and error announcements, focus management when results load, reduced-motion preferences, and status communication that never relies on colour alone. Maintain readable contrast and clear language throughout dense evidence and report sections.
